# Zenflow OS — Jobs-to-be-Done Pitch Notes

*Framework: Christensen's JTBD (Competing Against Luck). Written for the hackathon deck, demo script, and README lead.*

## The Job Statement

> **When I sit down to work with a day's worth of scattered tasks and fluctuating energy, I want something to decide what I should focus on right now and hold me in that focus, so I can end the day knowing the hours went somewhere.**

No product named — the job exists whether or not Zenflow does. The same person on a high-energy morning and a post-lunch slump has *different* jobs; that's why the coach asks for energy (1–10) before planning, and why plans differ.

## Three Dimensions

| Dimension | The customer's need | Where Zenflow serves it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Functional** | Turn "everything I could do" into "the one thing I'm doing now" | AI coach builds an energy-aware schedule and pushes it into the task board; pinned task + Pomodoro engine hold the focus |
| **Emotional** | Feel that the day is under control, not chasing me | One calm workspace instead of six tabs; streaks and stats prove progress happened |
| **Social** | Be seen (and see myself) as someone with a system, not someone winging it | The "OS" framing — your focus setup looks intentional, almost professional |

## Forces of Progress

- **Push** — the scattered-tools frustration: tasks in one app, timer in another, ambient sound in a YouTube tab, and no one deciding what's next. Every context switch leaks focus.
- **Pull** — one workspace where an AI plans around *your* energy and *your* open tasks, and the timer, sounds, and stats all talk to each other.
- **Anxiety** — "another productivity app I'll abandon"; "do I need an API key?"; "will my data leave my browser?" → Answered: works instantly with zero setup (hosted AI, no key needed), everything persists locally, nothing to configure before value appears.
- **Habit** — "my current mess works well enough." → Defeated by cost-of-entry: it's a URL. No signup, no import, no commitment. Try one focus session.

The demo script should spend as much time disarming Anxiety and Habit ("open the link, press Space, you're focusing") as showing Pull.

## Big Hire vs. Little Hire

- **Big Hire** (judge opens the URL / user bookmarks it): won by the first 30 seconds — the OS shell impresses, the coach generates a real plan without any key.
- **Little Hire** (coming back tomorrow): won by the loop — yesterday's streak is on the stats screen, the pinned task remembers your pomodoro count, your sound mix and wallpaper survived the reload. Persistence is not a checkbox feature; it *is* the retention mechanism.
- Demo both: generate a plan (Big), then reload the page mid-session to show everything survives (Little). That reload moment is the most underrated 10 seconds of the demo.

## Non-Obvious Competition

Zenflow is not competing with other Pomodoro apps. The real hires for this job:

1. **Non-consumption** — just opening the editor and hoping focus happens (the biggest competitor)
2. **The workaround stack** — Todoist + a phone timer + lo-fi YouTube + a paper list
3. **Willpower and guilt** — the zero-tooling incumbent
4. **Slack/social media** — what actually gets hired during the 3pm energy dip

Positioning line: **"Stop duct-taping four tabs together to protect your focus — hire one workspace whose job is exactly that."**

## Copy for the README / deck lead

Zenflow OS is an AI focus coach living inside its own desktop: it reads your energy and your open tasks, plans your next focus blocks, starts the timer, sets the soundscape, and proves at the end of the day that the hours went somewhere.
